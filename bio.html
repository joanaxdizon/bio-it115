JOANA DIZON

About me

Joana is a recent graduate of the University of Washington, where she completed a Bachelor of Science in Public Health-Global Health. During her time in college, she was part of a professional health fraternity, a student-led editorial, the campus' peer health educators, as well as numerous other leadership committees. Joana is currently in her first quarter of the Year Up program, where she hopes to learn how to leverage technology to improve health systems and address health disparities in marginalized communities.

Experiences

She is a passionate public health professional seeking to expand her knowledge and exposure to the various health services that promote equity and health in all communities. Experiences include health advocacy, case management, community outreach, and data collection and reporting. Eager to enhance her knowledge of the different systems and processes governing population health surveillance, community building and mobilization of stakeholders, and implementation of interventions.

Hobbies

In her free time, you can find Joana playing with her niece and nephews or whipping up a meal with her family. She grew up in the Pacific Northwest and enjoys being outdoors, hiking, paddle boarding, or running, when the weather permits it. Her ultimate pleasure is traveling the world and visiting places for long stretches of time, in order to really immerse herself in the culture and community. If she had all the time and money in the world, she would travel the world learning to cook various cuisines through local people and documenting her experiences through a blog.
